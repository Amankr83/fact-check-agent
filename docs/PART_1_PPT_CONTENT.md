## Deck Title
**CiteWise: Turning AI Search Visibility Into Revenue-Ready GEO Intelligence**

**Audience:** Product leadership, marketing leadership, founders, and interview panels evaluating product strategy depth.

**Core Thesis:** Search is moving from ranked links to synthesized answers. Brands now need a GEO operating system that explains not only whether they appear in AI answers, but why they are cited, which proof sources drive trust, and what actions will move visibility, sentiment, and revenue.

**Slide Objective:** Establish why GEO is urgent now.  
**Slide Content:**

- Google AI Overviews expanded to 200+ countries and 40+ languages; recent research found AIOs generated for 51.5% of representative real-user queries.
- GEO metrics now matter alongside SEO: brand mention rate, prompt share, citation frequency, sentiment, and competitive answer position.
- Executive implication: if AI engines do not cite the brand, the brand can disappear from high-intent consideration journeys.
**Visual Suggestion:** Full-width market shift graphic: "Blue links" → "AI answers" → "AI recommendations" → "Agentic actions."

## Slide 3

**Slide Title:** User Behavior Has Shifted From Clicking to Asking  
**Slide Objective:** Explain how behavior changed and why brands need GEO.  
**Slide Content:**
- Users now ask natural-language, high-context prompts such as "best CRM for a 50-person SaaS team" instead of searching keywords.
- AI answers compress discovery, evaluation, comparison, and trust formation into one interaction.
- Pew research reported lower click-through when AI summaries appear: users clicked result links in 8% of visits with AI summaries versus 15% without them.
- AI engines can cite sources outside the traditional top organic results, weakening the assumption that SEO rank alone guarantees visibility.
**Visual Suggestion:** Funnel compression diagram: Search → Read → Compare → Decide collapses into "Ask AI → Decide."  
**Speaker Notes:** The strategic risk is not only traffic loss. It is losing the recommendation moment before the website visit ever happens.

## Slide 4

**Slide Title:** Brands Face a Measurement Gap, Not Just a Content Gap  
**Slide Objective:** Define the current pain points.  
**Slide Content:**
- AI answers are non-deterministic: the same prompt can produce different citations and sentiment across runs.
- Marketing teams lack prompt-level diagnostics: they know rankings, but not which AI questions they win or lose.
- Citation quality is opaque: teams cannot easily see which third-party pages shape AI trust.
- Brand facts can be outdated, missing, or misattributed across models.
- Competitors can win prompts through earned media, comparison pages, forums, and review ecosystems even when their owned SEO footprint is weaker.
**Visual Suggestion:** Problem tree: "AI visibility blind spot" branching into measurement, source, sentiment, recommendation, and governance failures.  
**Speaker Notes:** The market is ready for workflow intelligence, not another dashboard that only reports mentions.

## Slide 10

**Slide Title:** CiteWise Workflow Converts AI Answers Into Actionable Recommendations  
**Slide Objective:** Explain feature workflow and components.  
**Slide Content:**

**Workflow**
1. User selects brand, competitors, markets, personas, and prompt clusters.
2. System runs multi-engine prompt tests across scheduled intervals.
3. Responses are parsed for mentions, rank/position, sentiment, citations, and claims.
4. Citation graph maps cited pages, domains, entities, freshness, and claim coverage.
5. Recommendation engine identifies gaps: missing proof, weak entity consistency, outdated pages, absent third-party validation.
6. User receives prioritized actions with predicted lift, confidence, effort, and owner.
7. System tracks post-action visibility and citation lift.

**Components**
- Data Inputs: prompts, brand entities, competitor set, webpages, citations, CRM/analytics events.
- AI Processing Layer: entity resolution, citation graph, semantic claim matching, confidence model.
- Outputs: opportunity score, recommendation cards, alert feed, executive report.
- Edge Cases: hallucinated citations, contradictory sources, model variance, regional differences, blocked crawlers.
**Visual Suggestion:** Architecture flow with a feedback loop from outcomes back into the recommendation model.  
**Speaker Notes:** The key is not one prompt result. It is repeated evidence over time.
